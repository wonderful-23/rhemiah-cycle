Understand Your Flow

Awareness before tracking. Education before calculation.

What Is Menstrual Flow?

Menstrual flow is the body’s natural way of releasing the uterine lining when pregnancy does not occur. It is a healthy, recurring process guided by hormonal changes.

Flow looks different for everyone — and it can change from month to month. Learning how your body responds helps replace worry with understanding.

Light, Medium & Heavy Flow

These descriptions are for awareness, not diagnosis.

Light Flow

Gentle bleeding or spotting. Often seen at the beginning or end of a cycle.

Medium Flow

Steady and predictable. Many people experience this as their usual rhythm.

Heavy Flow

More noticeable bleeding that may require frequent changes. For some bodies, this is a natural pattern.

What Affects Your Flow?

Your cycle reflects more than biology — it responds to daily life.

  • Stress and emotional load
  • Hormonal shifts
  • Sleep, nutrition, and movement
  • Age and life stage

Tips & Remedies for Comfortable Flow

These are gentle suggestions for comfort. Always consult healthcare providers for medical concerns.

🩸 Track Your Flow

Use period tracking apps or journals to understand your patterns and prepare accordingly.

🌿 Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water to help maintain flow consistency and reduce discomfort.

🛏️ Rest When Needed

Honor your body's signals. Rest during heavier flow days and adjust your schedule.

🍎 Nutrition Matters

Eat iron-rich foods like leafy greens, beans, and nuts to support your body's needs.

🧘 Gentle Movement

Light yoga, walking, or stretching can help with cramps and improve circulation.

💊 Natural Remedies

Consider herbal teas, heating pads, or over-the-counter remedies for comfort (consult your doctor).

What Is Normal?

Normal is personal. A healthy cycle is one that feels familiar to you over time.

Noticing changes is part of self-awareness — and seeking support when something feels unusual is a form of care, not concern.
